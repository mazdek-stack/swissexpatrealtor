Housing & Apartments

Finding Your Perfect Swiss Home

The timeline varies based on several factors:

  • Average Timeline: 4-8 weeks from search start to signed lease
  • Quick Searches (2-4 weeks): Flexible requirements, good availability, immediate start
  • Extended Searches (6-12 weeks): Very specific requirements, competitive areas, peak seasons
  • Peak Seasons: Spring/summer are busiest; winter often has more availability

Our personal network often helps us find homes faster than typical market timelines. We recommend starting your search 2-3 months before your desired move-in date.

Swiss landlords typically require:

  • Valid Permit: Work permit, residence permit, or employment contract (for EU/EFTA citizens)
  • Proof of Income: Employment contract showing salary sufficient to cover rent (usually rent should be max 1/3 of gross income)
  • Credit Check: Clean credit history
  • Rental Deposit: 2-3 months' rent, usually held in blocked account
  • Personal Liability Insurance: Many landlords require personal liability insurance covering tenant damages
  • References: Previous landlord and current employer references

We guide you through gathering these documents and present your application professionally.

Switzerland's rental market is highly competitive, especially in major cities:

  • Popular properties may receive 20-50+ applications
  • Viewings are often scheduled within days of listing
  • Best properties rarely appear on public portals
  • Language barriers can disadvantage international applicants
  • Local references and connections matter significantly

Our advantage: We leverage our personal network, local language skills, and professional reputation to significantly increase your acceptance odds.

Most permanent rentals in Switzerland are unfurnished, which typically means:

  • Included: Kitchen (cabinets, sink, appliances), bathroom fixtures
  • Not Included: All furniture, curtains, light bulbs
  • Variation: Standards vary - some include full kitchens, others only basic fixtures

Alternatives:

  • We can find furnished apartments (less common, higher rent, shorter leases)
  • Our temporary housing service offers fully furnished options while you search
  • We provide through our partner interior design services to help you furnish your new home
